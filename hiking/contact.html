Hiking Safety Guidelines

Stay safe on the trails with these essential tips and best practices

Essential Gear

  • Appropriate footwear with good traction
  • Weather-appropriate clothing (layers recommended)
  • Plenty of water (at least 2 liters for half-day hikes)
  • High-energy snacks and food
  • Navigation tools (map, compass, GPS)
  • First aid kit
  • Sun protection (hat, sunscreen, sunglasses)
  • Headlamp or flashlight with extra batteries
  • Emergency whistle
  • Fire starter and matches in waterproof container

Before You Go

  • Research your trail and know the route
  • Check weather forecasts and trail conditions
  • Inform someone of your plans and expected return time
  • Be realistic about your physical abilities
  • Start early to avoid hiking in darkness
  • Check for any permits or regulations
  • Prepare for changing weather conditions
  • Download offline maps if cell service is unreliable
  • Charge your phone fully
  • Consider hiking with a partner or group

On the Trail

  • Stay on marked trails
  • Pace yourself and take regular breaks
  • Stay hydrated and eat regularly
  • Be aware of your surroundings
  • Practice Leave No Trace principles
  • Cross streams carefully
  • Be cautious on steep or slippery terrain
  • Know wildlife safety for your area
  • Turn back if conditions worsen or you feel unsafe
  • Yield to uphill hikers

Emergency Situations

  • Stay calm and assess the situation
  • If lost, stop, stay put, and signal for help
  • Use your whistle: three short blasts is a universal distress signal
  • For injuries, apply appropriate first aid
  • If you need to shelter in place, find a protected area
  • Conserve your phone battery for emergency calls
  • Know how to signal rescue aircraft
  • In severe weather, seek appropriate shelter
  • Call 911 in life-threatening emergencies
  • Know the symptoms of hypothermia and heat exhaustion
